    Thesis: Virtuo

    Motion Graphics UX / UI Environmental

    In the field of cyberpsychology, there's a phenomenon called the Disappearing Computer Phenomenon, which posits that computers won't be objects, but built into the walls around us. My thesis for the MICA Post-Baccalaureate Graphic Design exhibition Impossibility features Virtuo. See how I imagine future computers as large as walls and how the future user leverages gesture technology.

    Inspiration

    In winter 2008, I visited the Haus der Musik in Vienna, Austria. One of the exhibits included an interactive game in which visitors could act as conductors with an orchestra on-screen. What if we could imagine interfaces on a grander scale? The way conductors can lead different instrumentalists on stage is the way a user can lead different applications on a computer. Conductors use fluid gestures to create music similar to the way users can compile research and more.

    Sketches

    I sketched, researched, and created storyboards for the final video. Before the project, I knew how to use Adobe Premiere, but I also had to learn how to use After Effects since I had to combine live action with animation.

    Storyboard 1

    One of the most difficult challenges was finalizing the storyline. Originally, I thought of two users working together on a project like planning a New Years Eve Party. One user would focus on logistics while the other user focused on aesthetics. This story line proved to be problematic since the scenario was too specific and was more relatable to public relations specialists.

    Storyboard 2

    In order to clearly convey how the interface would be useful to the average user of the future, I changed the storyline to feature five users: 2 callers, 1 music listener, 1 business man, and 1 designer. With this storyline, there was more room for flexibility in collaboration.

    Shooting Green Screen

    The project shooting actors with green screen and learning how to shoot video. I recruited fellow designers and students at MICA to be actors for my video.

    The Interface

    I decided to name the interface "Virtuo," taking inspiration from the interactive exhibit from the Haus der Musik. Virtuo is short from the word "virtuoso," meaning one who is skilled in music. Navigating and utilizing the interface involves gestures from conductors and from every day life.

      •

    Impossibility Exhibition

    I created the video to be displaying on one of the largest walls of the Fox 3 Gallery on MICA's campus. The projection enabled many visitors at a time to imagine the interface. The exhibition took place on March 27th to April 12th 2015.
