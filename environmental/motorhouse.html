    The Motorhouse

    The Motorhouse is an existing building in north Baltimore that has stood since its beginnings in the early 20th century. Like Baltimore, it has adapted over the decades, starting as a car sales building and transforming into a building full of independent artists.

      •

    The History

    Built in the 20th century, the Motor House began as a car sales shop. From 2005 until 2012, the same building on 120 W. North Avenue became "Load of Fun" for artists' studios.

    The Motorhouse Exterior

    The goal was to design wayfinding for The Motor House located on 120 West North Avenue in Baltimore, taking into account the visibility from pedestrian and automobile view.

    Inspiration

    Due to the building's extensive history, I took inspiration from industrial and contemporary designs. The inspiration and the history helped me realize a design that expresses durability and adaptability.

      •

    Sketch

    In order to account for daily traffic, I created designs that focused on viewers coming from the east side of North Avenue. Pedestrians and drivers can easily identify the Motorhouse from various blade and corner signs.

    Exterior Design

    To have a somewhat retro feel, I decided to use Gotham as the typeface. With aluminum-finished signage, I wanted to create a design that doesn't fight with the brick, but exposes the brick.

    Signage Materials

    Signs will be made of steel laid with an aluminum finish similar to what you see below. The signage will be bolted and drilled into the brick for stability. Again, the materials allude to the industrial and adaptive history of the building.
